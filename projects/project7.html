Mapping for Success

Concept:

​An app akin to Uber – curating and mapping on­demand opportunities based on geography, and identifying where resources can meet families’ needs locally.

Need:

​​Classrooms are full – and refugees are challenged to find jobs in the formal economy. A service-­point app will allow refugee children and their families to access services and matches workers with sharing economy employment opportunities. For example, a Turkish driver has room in his car, he might be willing to drive children to school if compensated, or provided a subsidy from the government. This app allows temporary to permamnent employment by presenting a gig economy­-like model for unemployed refugees.

Potential Partners:

Tech companies Yelp, TaskRabbit, Uber, Airbnb and other sharing economy companies.

Solution(s) Proposed:

​​A Yelp­-meets-­Taskrabbit-­meets-Uber­-meets­-Airbnb for overcoming obstacles to refugee education, a modular offering that would adapt to geographies and regional needs.

Key Milestones:

  • ​​Bandwidth and data access in remote areas; universality of access and functionality. Identifying and securing funding streams where peer­-to­-peer payments may not be feasible.
  • Ensure the app takes into consideration different locations of schools. Text-­based systems will work better as smartphones and data plans won’t necessarily be commonplace.

Key questions:

  • Are there any existing text­based platforms that have executed this model successfully?
  • What companies might be compelled to partner to bring this to life?
