Concept:

​A platform Employment ­ready individuals addressing the needs of employers as well as the skills of employees

Need:

​Preparing for what happens after education, particularly for apprentice workers. Any platform must include the ability to work (outsourced work, e­work, remote work, apprenticeships) but any intervention must be l​egal​ ensure safety from being deported into planning for this.

Potential Partners:

​Linkedin, Microsoft, Google, S​yrians or Arabs in the diaspora who could help with language training, advocating for fellow Syrians among employers in regions.

Solution(s) Proposed:

Not overriding existing initiatives Syrians are undertaking,but bringing those initiatives onboard onto the same platforms, and making any employment platform cross­compatible with their existing efforts. Any initiative must address and come packaged with a clear policy framework ­ reflecting the nation­specific challenges in the employment of Syrian refugees ­ and an effort to support NGOs that are already with refugees in areas of employment. The focus will need to include onboarding existing workers onto this platform.
