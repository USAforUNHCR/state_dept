Refugee Resource Hub Application and Site

Concept:

​A hub to provide access to and information around services beyond language services ­– building beyond 4W’s mapping of services – a real­time location­ based digital and mobile waypoint for refugees and service providers.

Need:

​Services offerings are so disperse and difficult to understand refugees need a simple, universally understandable means to access and connect various services at a local level.

Potential Partners:

U​niversities in computer science; Google, UNHCR in oversight of refugee services in various countries where organizations are operating.

Solution(s) Proposed:

​​A complete app and digital suite, easily accessible across platforms, the solution itself would allow refugees themselves to provide feedback in­ the app interface. The Yelp­-like suite of services available for refugees would allow refugees or service providers to search by type of service and geographic area; this resource might also include employment tools and crawl existing resources to surface employment opportunities.

Key Milestones:

  • ​Local NGOs, international NGOs provide connected detail around the types of work and services they have built on the ground. Applying a "period of performance" will ensure all content remains relevant. Information would expire within and appropriate time horizon.
  • Notes: ICT for Programs has a service point app (IRC); via UNHCR Innovation, help.unhcr.org​ can potentially help keep content relevant and current.

Key questions:

  • What organizations have bandwidth to develop this tool?
  • What services are the necessary, core services to include in this app?
  • Are there any organizations or actors with services operating with location­based databases to incorporate into this initiative?
