Solution: Employment Platform

Concept:

​A platform for Employment-­ready individuals addressing the needs of employers as well as the skills of employees

Need:

​Preparing for what happens after education, particularly for apprentice workers. Any platform must include the ability to work (outsourced work, e­work, remote work, apprenticeships) but any intervention must be l​egal​ within the host countries, to ensure participant's safety.

Potential Partners:

​Linkedin, Microsoft, Google, as critical technology partners; those in the diaspora who could aid in language training, advocating for fellow Syrians among employers in regions.

Solution(s) Proposed:

Bringing existing initiatives in Syria onboard onto a master platform, and making any employment platform cross­-compatible with existing efforts. Any initiative must address and come packaged with a clear policy framework ­reflecting the nation-­specific challenges in the employment of Syrian refugees, and an effort to support NGOs that are already workin with refugees in areas of employment.

Key Milestones:

  • ​Bring onboard Nabbesh or a similar entity ­representing existing platforms or a mentor matching platform to­ work with stakeholders and allocate funds.
  • ​Notes: Microsoft can potentially provide capabilities/services for platform. Nations like Jordan allow legal work, and likely provide a policy framework to replicate this.

Key questions:

  • Who or what organization has resources that can provide teacher language training?
  • What are existing organizations activating this work?
  • Who will lead the content development and sustain the platform?
  • How are these tools distributed? What on­the­ground practitioners have capabilities to distribute any teacher training resources?
