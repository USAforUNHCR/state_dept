Refugee Resource Hub Application and Site

Concept:

​A hub to provide access to and information around services beyond language services ­– building beyond 4W’s mapping of services – a real­time location­based digital and mobile waypoint for refugees and service providers.

Need:

​Services offerings are so disperse and difficult to understand refugees need a simple, universally understandable means to access and connect various services at a local level.

Partners:

U​niversities in computer science, Google, UNHCR in oversight of refugee services in various countries where organizations are operating.

Solution(s) Proposed:

​​A complete app and digital suite, easily accessible across platforms, the solution itself would allow refugees themselves to provide feedback in­app. The Yelp­like app of services that are available for refugees would allow refugees or service providers to search by type of service and area; this resource might also include employment tools and crawl existing resources to surface employment opportunities.

Key Milestones:

  • ​ocal NGOs, international NGOs could all provide information on the types of work they’re doing. Applying a period of performance would ensure all content remains relevant information would expire when project finished
  • Next Steps: ​Identify existing capabilities and organizational bandwidth to develop such an app.
  • Notes: ICT for Programs has a service point app (IRC); via UNHCR Innovation, help.unhcr.org​ would be great to keep information current; country based resource for reference

Key questions:

  • What organizations have bandwidth to develop this tool?
  • What services are the necessary, core services to include in this app?
  • Are there any organizations or actors with services operating with location­based databases to incorporate into this initiative?
