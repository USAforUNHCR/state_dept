Solution: Virtual Distance Teacher Training

Concept:

​An attempt to address the issue of psychosocial challenges many children experience, not merely due to conflict alone but due to prevalent violence and bullying in school. This project will create a teacher training tool and curricula that empowers teachers to foster inclusion, address trauma ­and work in tandem with teachers to equip them to meet their needs and the evolving realities of the classroom.

Need:

Make available nonexistent curricula and leverage as a joint employment and training tool. These communities need virtual training programs, and broader, connected communities of practice with other teachers in their region. Face to Face and virtual interaction are key to build cohorts of support.

Potential Partners:

​Varki Foundation’s master teacher training program; explore leveraging existing partnerships within the Teacher’s College at Columbia and online training videos from the Teaching Channel.

Solution(s) Proposed:

​A​​m​ulti­pronged approach, working from the ground up to make this platform available to teachers.

Key Milestones:

  • Engaging in partnerships with ministries of education to more formally integrate into schools.
  • Notes: Activate U​niversity of the People a​s a key resource for this initiative
  • STIR in India (Raoul @ Malala) – a similar model focused on teachers training together and maintaining capacity building through networks.

Key questions:
