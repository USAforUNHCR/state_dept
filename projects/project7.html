Mapping for Success

Concept:

​An app akin to Uber – curating and mapping on­demand opportunities based on geography, and identifying where resources can meet families’ needs locally.

Need:

​​Classrooms are full – and refugees are challenged to find jobs in the formal economy. A service­point app that allows refugee children and their families to access services and matches workers with sharing economy employment opportunities. For example, a Turkish driver has room in his car, willing to drive children to a school if compensated with a subsidy from the government. Identifies and allows employment by presenting a gig economy­like model for unemployed refugees.

Potential Partners:

Tech companies Yelp, TaskRabbit, Uber, Airbnb and other sharing economy companies.

Solution(s) Proposed:

​​A Yelp­ meets ­Taskrabbit ­meets ­Uber­ meets­ Airbnb for overcoming obstacles to refugee education, a modular offering that would adapt to geographies and regional needs.

Key Milestones:

  • ​​Bandwidth and data access in remote areas; universality. Funding streams where peer­-to­-peer payments may not be feasible. Funding streams where credit cards might not be available.
  • Ensure the app takes into consideration different locations of schools ­- one in the university outside the refugee camps, while one is in the city. How will they get children to a private school on private property? How will this address potential challenges working with the Jordanian government? Text­based systems will work better as smartphones and data plans won’t necessarily be commonplace.

Key questions:

  • Are there any existing text­based platforms that have executed this model successfully?
  • What companies might be compelled to partner to bring this to life?
