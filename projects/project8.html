Mapping Tool; Tangible Mobile Classroom; Mobile App to Enable Homework Reinforcement

Concept:

Leveraging existing technology, bring the classroom to refugee students who cannot easily access their school or who lack the technology to access an education digitally.

Need:

R​efugee families often live on the outskirts of school districts and communities. This app or offering would use a mapping tool ­- RGIS - to identify outer neighborhoods where refugees cannot easily attend school.

Potential Partners:

​Mobile classroom ­- Stanford d.school’s Spark Truck brings school teachers to communities with streamlined curricula. The app Rethink Education, a startup based in South Africa, delivers an online platform to compete assignments. It allows students to sell education data as an incentive for doing homework.

Solution(s) Proposed:

​​A Yelp­ meets ­Taskrabbit ­meets ­Uber­ meets­ Airbnb for overcoming obstacles to refugee education, a modular offering that would adapt to geographies and regional needs.

Key Milestones:

  • ​​The initiative might harness social media like Kickstarter, Indiegogo and YouTube to initially fundraise. The notion of achieving scale and inspire and support a greater number of social enterprises.
  • Next Steps: ​Initial coordination, funding challenges; Identifying philanthropists and foundations to support this work.
  • Notes:​A partnership between UK Govt, UNICEF, UNHCR ­- H​umanitarian Education Accelerator​­ looking for smaller scale projects ready to go to scale. The mobile classroom should incorporate a blended learning program – prioritizing online learning activities combined with blended learning.

Key questions:

  • What organizations can support this initiative both in creating mobile classrooms as an employment opportunity for refugees as well as developing curricula?
  • What technologies beyond RGIS will be needed for this initiative?
